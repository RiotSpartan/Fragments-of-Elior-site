Property Overview

Tucked deep within the wooded countryside of Hollow Creek County, Blackthorn Estate stands as one of the region's oldest surviving residential properties. Built in the late 19th century, the estate combines historic architecture, expansive living space, and unmatched privacy across several secluded acres. Utilities Active.

Interior Details

The residence includes multiple private quarters, a formal dining area, sitting rooms, a dedicated office space, an upper-level children's wing, and a private study containing preserved records from previous ownership. Original woodwork, preserved stone detailing, and custom interior craftsmanship remain largely untouched.

Included With Sale

Large portions of the estate have been preserved in their original condition, offering a rare opportunity for restoration enthusiasts, private collectors, or buyers seeking a secluded retreat away from modern development. Property includes archived records, personal belongings left by previous occupants, and various locked storage areas to be transferred as-is to the future owner.

Known Conditions

  • Portions of the property may require structural attention due to estate age
  • Intermittent electrical instability has been reported in isolated sections of the home
  • The property has remained vacant for an extended period of time
  • Nighttime tours are unavailable; serious inquiries only
  • Entry hall acoustics may produce footsteps from unoccupied floors
  • Master bedroom door has been repaired three times since vacancy
  • Study includes built-in shelving, locked storage, and sealed wall damage
  • Some mirrors, portraits, and nursery items are excluded from removal

Disclosure Notice

Property is associated with a previously documented residential incident involving former occupants of the estate. Due to the condition and age of archived investigative materials, portions of the historical record remain incomplete or unavailable.

Certain areas of the residence were temporarily restricted during the original investigation and subsequent estate processing procedures. Restrictions have since been lifted.

Buyer acknowledges purchase of property in current condition and accepts all remaining contents, records, and archived materials as-is.
