About the Game

Step Into the Story

You weren’t supposed to stay long.

The house was just another listing — quiet, abandoned, and worth more than it should be. But something about it feels wrong. The deeper you go, the more the silence begins to shift.

Fragments of Elior is a psychological horror experience where you step into the role of Felix, a realtor drawn into a home with a past that refuses to stay buried. What begins as a routine walkthrough slowly becomes something far more unsettling, as the house reveals pieces of a fractured family and a presence that never truly left.

This is not just a story told through dialogue. It is told through the environment, through what remains, and through what should not still be there. Every room holds a piece of the truth. Every discovery pulls you deeper into the mystery surrounding Elior.

Some things in this house want to be found. Others do not.

Character Preview

The house remembers them all.

Felix Carter

Felix is a young realtor drawn to a property that feels… off from the moment he arrives. Logical and observant, he approaches the mansion with a sense of routine and control—but that sense begins to fracture as the house reveals itself.

What starts as a simple showing becomes something far more personal. The deeper Felix explores, the harder it becomes to separate what is real from what the mansion wants him to see.

Elior

Elior is a young boy whose presence lingers within the mansion, felt more than seen. Quiet and innocent, he remains tied to the home through scattered traces—forgotten toys, silent rooms, and a worn teddy bear that never seems to stay where it was left.

As the truth within the house begins to surface, Elior's story becomes impossible to ignore. Whether he is a memory, a guide, or something more, his connection to the mansion runs deeper than it first appears.

Marcus

Marcus is a dedicated provider who spent much of his life working to support his family, often at the cost of being present within it. Practical and focused, he approached life with structure and responsibility—but something within the mansion began to erode that stability.

Though he sought to maintain control, the weight of what unfolded inside the home proved too great. What remains of Marcus is not just memory, but a quiet echo of a man who tried to hold everything together as it slipped beyond his grasp.

Julia

Julia is a woman shaped by a past she cannot outrun, her presence within the mansion marked by tension and quiet unrest. Guarded and distant, she carries a resentment that lingers beneath the surface, influencing both her actions and the atmosphere around her.

As the truth within the house begins to surface, Julia’s role becomes increasingly difficult to ignore. Whether driven by regret, anger, or something deeper, her connection to the events within the mansion runs far deeper than it first appears.

Blanche

Blanche is a gentle and nurturing presence within the home, offering warmth in a place that otherwise feels cold and unsettled. Patient and understanding, she represents a sense of comfort that seems increasingly out of place as the mansion reveals its true nature.

Even as darkness takes hold, traces of Blanche’s kindness remain woven into the environment. In a house filled with tension and silence, her presence stands as one of the last reminders of what once felt like home.

The deeper one ventures into the house, the more that presence begins to press against reality itself. What it is—or what it wants—remains unknown.
